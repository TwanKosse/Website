A Journey Through Time

Ediacaran Cambrian Ordovician Silurian Devonian Carboniferous Permian Triassic Jurassic Cretaceous Paleogene Neogene Quaternary

Ediacaran

635-541 MYA

The Ediacaran Period, marked a pivotal time in Earth's history. It witnessed the emergence of complex multicellular life forms, known as the Ediacara biota, which thrived in marine environments. Notably, these organisms lacked hard shells or skeletons, showcasing a unique evolutionary experiment before the explosion of diverse life forms in the Cambrian Period.

The Stars

Cambrian

541-485 MYA

The Cambrian Period, is renowned as the "Cambrian Explosion" due to the sudden proliferation of diverse and complex life forms. This period witnessed the emergence of almost all major animal groups, including the first appearance of many modern phyla. Fossils from this time provide crucial insights into the early evolution of life on Earth.

The Stars

Ordovician

485-443 MYA

The Ordovician Period, was characterized by a flourishing marine biodiversity following the Cambrian Explosion. It witnessed the proliferation of various marine organisms, including early fish, corals, and cephalopods. Additionally, extensive diversification of life occurred in both shallow and deep sea environments, marking a significant evolutionary milestone in Earth's history.

The Stars

Silurian

443-419 MYA

The Silurian Period, was marked by significant geological and biological events. It saw the continued diversification of marine life, including the emergence of jawed fish and early terrestrial plants. Land colonization by plants intensified, leading to the formation of complex ecosystems. Additionally, this period witnessed major changes in global climate and sea levels.

The Stars

Devonian

419-359 MYA

The Devonian Period, is often called the "Age of Fishes" due to the diversification of fish species, including the first appearance of jawed and armored fish. It witnessed the spread of forests on land and the evolution of early amphibians. Additionally, this period saw the formation of vast reef systems and the rise of complex ecosystems.

The Stars

Carboniferous

359-299 MYA

The Carboniferous Period, is renowned for its lush forests, which gave rise to extensive coal deposits, hence its name. It witnessed the dominance of amphibians and early reptiles, along with the emergence of diverse insect species. Additionally, this period experienced significant fluctuations in global climate, including glaciations and warmer intervals.

The Stars

Permian

299-252 MYA

The Permian Period, was characterized by the assembly of the supercontinent Pangaea. It saw the flourishing of diverse terrestrial ecosystems, including early reptiles and synapsids, the ancestors of mammals. However, the period ended with the largest mass extinction event in Earth's history, wiping out around 96% of marine species and 70% of terrestrial vertebrate species.

The Stars

Triassic

252-201 MYA

The Triassic Period, marked the recovery and diversification of life following the Permian-Triassic mass extinction. It witnessed the emergence of dinosaurs as dominant terrestrial animals, alongside the proliferation of early mammals, reptiles, and archosaurs. Additionally, this period saw the breakup of Pangaea and the formation of distinct landmasses, shaping Earth's geography for millions of years to come.

The Stars

Jurassic

201-145 MYA

The Jurassic Period, is renowned for the dominance of dinosaurs as the ruling terrestrial vertebrates. Iconic species like Stegosaurus, Brachiosaurus, and Allosaurus roamed the Earth. Additionally, this period saw the emergence of early birds and the diversification of marine life, including ichthyosaurs, plesiosaurs, and ammonites, in ancient oceans.

The Stars

Cretaceous

145-66 MYA

The Cretaceous Period, witnessed the apex of dinosaur diversity, including giants like Tyrannosaurus rex and Triceratops. It saw the spread of flowering plants, leading to diverse terrestrial ecosystems. Additionally, this period ended with the devastating Cretaceous-Paleogene extinction event, resulting in the extinction of non-avian dinosaurs and the rise of mammals as dominant land animals.

The Stars

Paleogene

66-23 MYA

The Paleogene Period, marked a transition from the age of dinosaurs to the rise of modern mammals. It witnessed significant global climate change, including the gradual cooling of Earth's temperatures. Additionally, this period saw the diversification and expansion of mammalian species, leading to the emergence of many modern groups and ecosystems.

The Stars

Neogene

23-2.6 MYA

The Neogene Period, witnessed the evolution of modern ecosystems and the rise of familiar landscapes. It saw the continued diversification of mammals, including early hominids. Additionally, this period experienced significant climatic fluctuations, including the onset of ice ages, shaping the Earth's environment and influencing the distribution of species.

The Stars

Quaternary

2.6-0 MYA

The Quaternary Period, is distinguished by repeated glaciations and interglacial periods, shaping Earth's surface dramatically. It witnessed the evolution of Homo sapiens and the rise of modern human civilizations. Additionally, this period is marked by significant changes in global climate and the widespread impact of human activity on the environment.
